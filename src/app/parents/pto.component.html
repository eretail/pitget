THRS Partners, a volunteer organization at The Honor Roll School, consists of THRS parents, teachers and staff.

THRS Partners is a non-profit 501(c)(3) organization, which was formed for social, charitable, and educational causes. Our purpose is to seek, cultivate and obtain revenues to be used for developing and expanding educational opportunities for children who attend The Honor Roll School.

In past years, THRS Partners has used fundraising revenues to complete many projects on campus. such as commissioning a fantastic mural in the ECP lobby from Mr. Guillermo, Promethean Boards and overhead projections systems, a campus library expansion, computer lab and playground upgrades.  More recent projects included a campus-wide surveillance system,  the Front Lawn “re-do”, new sound and projection systems in the gym and the dining hall, and a broadcast studio with audio speakers in every classroom.  The THRS gymnasium was completely renovated. With new floor paint and design, coordinating wall paint, new curtain dividers and an easy-to-use electronic bleacher system, the home of the Fighting Dolphins has never looked better! Extensive improvements in the performing arts department and STEAM allow your students to be showcased. Most recently, proceeds from Festival of Friends 2017-18 were used to create a 21st century Innovation Lab. Our existing library was upgraded into a collaborative learning center,  integrating digital and print resources,  that’s right for today and for the future.

Throughout the year, we hold a series of events that showcase our school spirit such as Festival of Friends/Dolphin Dash Fun Run (on alternating years), Socials, Teacher Appreciations and Fun Food Fridays. We invite you to join us at any or all these events to see THRS SPIRIT in action!

How You Can Get Involved

Partners Volunteer Sign Up Form

Volunteerism is one of the most integral components of a successful relationship between a family and their school of choice. Studies have shown that children, whose parents regularly participate in their child’s school community, achieve a higher level of satisfaction and academic success than those whose parents do not. A parent’s regular attendance in their child’s school gives concrete proof to the children that education is indeed one of their families highest priorities.

Volunteering allows parents to develop deeper relationships between themselves and their children’s teachers and administrators. Whether you choose to volunteer weekly or once a year, there is an opportunity for every single parent to volunteer.
